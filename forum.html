The Conference

What is the
Focus Forum?

Focus Forum is a two-day event built for owners and leaders who carry the weight of growing organizations. If you possess the will to grow but are struggling with the way, this conference is for you.

This is not a large-scale, anonymous event. The Focus Forum began as a small group of business owners gathered to learn the principles of the Entrepreneurial Operating System and discuss real organizational challenges amongst peers. The 2025 gathering in St. Louis was so successful we immediately began planning the next — which you are cordially invited to.

"This is a working conversation with your entrepreneurial peers that emphasizes execution, leadership, and how to responsibly integrate A.I. technology in ways that practically help your business."

While You're Here

St. Louis Awaits

Arrive early. Stay late. The Gateway City has more than you'd expect.

01
Gateway Arch

Ride to the top of the country's tallest man-made monument. The view of the Mississippi River and downtown St. Louis is worth every second.

02
City Museum

Part playground, part art installation, part fever dream. An absolute must for anyone who thought they'd outgrown wonder. Bring your sense of adventure.

03
Forest Park

1,371 acres of free museums, trails, and open space. Home to the St. Louis Art Museum, the Zoo, and the History Museum — all free to the public.

04
Soulard

One of the oldest neighborhoods in America. Cobblestone streets, live blues, and a farmers market running since 1779. The city's soul in a single neighborhood.

05
The Hill

St. Louis's legendary Italian neighborhood. Old-school red sauce, bocce courts, and a sense of community your grandfather would recognize. Dinner on The Hill is mandatory.

06
Cardinals at Busch Stadium

June is peak baseball season. Catch a Cardinals game at Busch Stadium — one of the most beautiful parks in the National League. The city will be loud.

Giving Back

Our Charity

A portion of every ticket supports The Hope Center Indy — a faith-based organization on the front lines of restoring lives through comprehensive care.

Visit hopecenterindy.org →

The Hope Center Indy operates a 25-acre campus in Indianapolis serving women ages 18–30 coming out of sex trafficking, sexual exploitation, and addiction. Their mission: to impart hope and healing to every heart through programs that address both immediate needs and long-term restoration.

Their model goes beyond shelter. Through vocational training, therapy, and social enterprises — coffee shops and boutiques whose profits fund the mission — survivors are equipped for lives of independence and purpose. The past does not define you. That's not a tagline at Hope Center. It's the operating principle.
